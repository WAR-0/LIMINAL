## Synthesis Methodology Framework

### 1. Convergent Analysis
Identify areas where all three AI assessments reach similar conclusions:
- **High-confidence findings** (unanimous agreement + research foundation support)
- **Consensus concerns** (universal risk identification across assessments)  
- **Shared strategic recommendations** (convergent implementation guidance)
- **Consistent theoretical critiques** (unified scientific merit assessments)

### 2. Divergent Analysis  
Examine areas where AI assessments disagree or show uncertainty:
- **Assessment perspective differences** (analyze why AIs reached different conclusions)
- **Confidence level variations** (where assessments expressed different certainty levels)
- **Alternative valid interpretations** (multiple reasonable readings of evidence)
- **Methodology differences** (varying analytical approaches and their implications)

### 3. Meta-Assessment Quality Analysis
Evaluate the assessment process itself:
- **Methodological rigor** of each assessment approach
- **Evidence utilization effectiveness** (how comprehensively each used available research)
- **Analytical blind spots** (areas inadequately addressed by all three assessments)
- **Research integration quality** (how well assessments leveraged the 93+ research files)

### 4. Research Foundation Integration
Connect synthesis findings to the extensive research base:
- **Theoretical validation** (how well LIMINAL aligns with consciousness research findings)
- **Empirical gap identification** (areas where research doesn't support LIMINAL's approach)
- **Novel contribution assessment** (what LIMINAL adds beyond existing research)
- **Implementation risk factors** (validated through research literature)
