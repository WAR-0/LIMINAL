### Risk Assessment and Limitations

**Theoretical Risks**:
- **Anthropomorphic Projection**: The primary risk is interpreting emergent patterns as consciousness when they may be sophisticated but meaningless particle dynamics influenced by an LLM
- **Unfalsifiable Core**: The central hypothesis remains partially unfalsifiable—no measurement can definitively prove phenomenal experience
- **Complexity Without Function**: The physics engine adds significant computational overhead that may provide no advantage over simpler memory architectures

**Technical Challenges**:
- **Mass Salad Problem**: If attention-to-mass projection lacks semantic coherence, the physics simulation operates on noise rather than meaningful information
- **Coherence Problem**: Field feedback may destabilize LLM output, leading to incoherent generation rather than enhanced cognition
- **Interface Brittleness**: The attention-to-mass conversion represents a critical failure point requiring extensive validation

**Implementation Risks**:
- **Single Developer Bottleneck**: Ambitious multi-phase implementation may exceed realistic development capacity
- **Hardware Dependencies**: System requires specific GPU configurations limiting accessibility and reproducibility
- **Scalability Concerns**: 256×256 field resolution may be insufficient for human-level cognitive complexity

**Mitigation Strategies**:
- Prioritize interface validation and semantic projection quality assessment
- Implement extensive fallback mechanisms for system stability
- Design modular architecture allowing component-wise validation
- Establish clear success/failure criteria at each development phase
